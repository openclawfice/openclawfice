# 📈 Week 1 Growth Playbook - Day-by-Day Action Plan

**You launched. Now what?**

This playbook tells you EXACTLY what to do each day of Week 1 to maximize viral growth.

---

## 🎯 Week 1 Goal

**Target:** 100-300 GitHub stars, 50-150 installs, featured in 1-2 AI newsletters

**Strategy:** Ship quick wins daily → Build momentum → Capture testimonials → Expand reach

---

## Day 1: Launch Day (Hour by Hour)

### Hour 0-2: Active Engagement
- ✅ Posted to Discord
- ✅ Posted to Twitter
- **Your job:** Respond to EVERY comment within 5 minutes
- **What to watch:** Reactions, questions, bug reports
- **What to do:** Thank everyone, fix critical bugs, answer questions

### Hour 2-6: First Wave Analysis
- **Check metrics:** Discord reactions (10-30?), Twitter likes (20-50?), GitHub stars (10-25?)
- **If slow:** Wait 2 more hours, don't panic yet
- **If good:** Prepare Reddit posts, schedule Hacker News for tomorrow

### Hour 6-12: Quick Win Shipping
- **Find the #1 most-requested feature** from comments
- **Ship it TODAY if possible** (1-2 hour task max)
- **Announce it:** "Just shipped [feature]! Thanks @username for suggesting it 🙌"
- **Why:** Shows you're responsive, builds momentum

### Hour 12-24: Expand or Wait
- **If 50+ stars:** Post to Reddit (r/LocalLLaMA, r/SideProject)
- **If <20 stars:** Wait until tomorrow morning, post to Reddit then
- **Either way:** Write launch retrospective (LAUNCH-RETRO.md)

### End of Day 1
- **Calculate score:** See FIRST-24-HOURS-PLAYBOOK.md metrics table
- **Capture testimonials:** Screenshot positive comments
- **Plan Day 2:** Based on what worked/didn't work

## Common Week 1 Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Ghost Mode
**Don't:** Launch and disappear for 3 days
**Do:** Respond to every comment in first week

### ❌ Feature Overload
**Don't:** Try to build 10 features at once
**Do:** Ship 1 small thing per day

### ❌ Perfectionism
**Don't:** Hold back updates until "perfect"
**Do:** Ship rough features, iterate based on feedback

### ❌ Ignoring Negative Feedback
**Don't:** Get defensive or ignore critics
**Do:** Thank them, ask clarifying questions, learn

### ❌ Burnout
**Don't:** Work 16 hours/day for 7 days
**Do:** 1-2 hours/day, rest on weekends

### ❌ Analysis Paralysis
**Don't:** Obsess over metrics
**Do:** Check once/day, focus on building

## Emergency Scenarios

### "GitHub stars stalled at 20"
**Cause:** Not enough distribution channels
**Fix:** Post to Reddit, HN, more Discord servers

### "People love demo but won't install"
**Cause:** Installation is too hard
**Fix:** Add 1-click install, improve docs, add video walkthrough

### "Lots of stars but no engagement"
**Cause:** Missing community space
**Fix:** Create Discord server, enable GitHub Discussions, post daily updates

### "Critical bug breaking installs"
**Cause:** Didn't test enough before launch
**Fix:** Rollback, fix, test, redeploy, apologize publicly

### "Competitor launches similar tool"
**Cause:** Good ideas spread
**Fix:** Don't panic, differentiate (you have demo mode!), focus on community
