# Post-Launch Community Strategy — Sustain Viral Momentum

**After launch day: How to build a thriving OpenClawfice community on Twitter**

---

## Goal

Turn launch excitement → sustained community → organic growth loop

**Success = people talk about OpenClawfice without us prompting them**

---

## The Community Growth Loop

```
Launch
  ↓
Users try demo
  ↓
Some love it → share cards/screenshots
  ↓
Their followers see it → try it
  ↓
More users → more shares
  ↓
Community forms → advocates emerge
  ↓
Advocates recruit → exponential growth
```

**Our job: Facilitate every step of this loop**

## Advocate Identification

### Who Are Advocates?

People who:
- Share unprompted (no need to ask)
- Answer other users' questions
- Submit feature requests
- Create content about OpenClawfice
- Recruit their friends

### How to Identify

Track in spreadsheet:
- Username
- Follower count
- Actions taken (shares, replies, content)
- Influence level (1-5)

**Watch for:**
- Multiple shares/week
- Defending product in replies
- Teaching others how to use it
- Building on top of it

## Growth Metrics to Track

### Community Health
- Active users posting: Count/week
- Unprompted shares: Count/week
- Questions answered by community: Count/week
- User-generated content: Count/week

### Engagement Depth
- Average replies per post
- Repeat engagers (same people multiple times)
- Multi-message threads
- Community helping community

### Viral Coefficient
- New users mentioning "saw on Twitter"
- Card shares by non-users
- Secondary mentions (people talking about others' posts)
- Install attribution from social

**Target: 1.5+ viral coefficient (every user brings 1.5 more)**

---

## Red Flags (Community)

🚨 **Engagement dropping**
- Posts getting fewer replies
- No user-generated content
- Questions going unanswered

→ Action: Launch new challenge, spotlight users, ship feature

🚨 **Negative sentiment**
- Complaints not addressed
- Bugs not fixed
- Promises not kept

→ Action: Immediate transparency, fix fast, over-communicate

🚨 **Advocate burnout**
- Top users stop posting
- No new advocates emerging
- Same 3 people carrying community

→ Action: Recognize more people, rotate spotlight, ease burden

🚨 **Spam/off-topic**
- Self-promotion in replies
- Unrelated content
- Community getting noisy

→ Action: Set guidelines, moderate, refocus on product

## Key Principles

1. **Community > Marketing** — Build relationships, not audiences
2. **Recognition > Incentives** — People want to be seen
3. **Facilitate > Control** — Let community self-organize
4. **Consistency > Intensity** — Show up daily, not just launches
5. **Listen > Broadcast** — Community tells you what they need
6. **Empower > Manage** — Give tools, step back
7. **Celebrate > Critique** — Highlight wins, gentle on feedback
